# The Middle East erupts: US-Israeli strikes on Iran trigger region-wide war

**The United States and Israel launched a massive joint military offensive against Iran on the morning of February 28, 2026, triggering the most significant Middle East escalation since the 2003 Iraq invasion.** Iran retaliated within hours, firing 23 waves of ballistic missiles at Israel and striking at least 14 US military bases across the Gulf. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of global oil transits — has been effectively shut down, Houthi forces in Yemen announced they will resume Red Sea shipping attacks, and a coordinated cyberattack plunged Iran into a near-total internet blackout. Every major actor in the region is now activated or on high alert, and the conflict has expanded across at least seven countries in a single day.

## Operation "Epic Fury" struck hundreds of targets across Iran

The joint operation began at 8:15 AM Israel time (06:27 GMT) on February 28. Israel deployed roughly **200 fighter jets** — its largest sortie in history — while the US launched strikes from two carrier strike groups (USS Abraham Lincoln and USS Gerald R. Ford) using F-35Cs, F/A-18E Super Hornets, and EA-18G Growlers. Twelve F-22 Raptors, deployed to Ovda Airbase in southern Israel on February 24 in the first-ever US offensive weapons deployment on Israeli soil, also participated.

Israeli jets alone struck approximately **500 targets across 28 regions** throughout Iran. Targets in Tehran included the Ministry of Defense, Ministry of Intelligence, Atomic Energy Headquarters, Khamenei's residence compound, the Judiciary building, and the Presidential building. Nuclear facilities near Karaj and Qom were hit, along with IRGC Aerospace Forces installations in Tabriz, Kermanshah, Khorramabad, and Dezful, and IRGC Navy facilities across southern Iran.

President Trump claimed on Truth Social that Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed. NPR confirmed via an anonymous source that Khamenei died in an Israeli strike. **Iran has not confirmed his death** — Foreign Minister Araghchi told NBC that the supreme leader and president are alive, though he acknowledged "several commanders were killed." Iran's defense minister and IRGC chief were also reportedly killed. The Iranian Red Crescent Society reported over **200 killed** across Iran, including at least 40 in a strike that hit an elementary girls' school in Minab, Hormozgan province. Trump stated the objectives were eliminating Iran's nuclear and missile programs, destroying its navy, and changing its leadership, urging Iranians to "take over your government."

## Iran's retaliation spans seven countries

Iran responded with an operation dubbed **"Sincere Promise 4,"** launching 23 waves of missile attacks at Israel by 5 PM local time. Eight salvos targeted northern Israel, nine targeted the Tel Aviv area, three hit Jerusalem, and three struck southern Israel. Reports describe enormous damage in Haifa from two direct impacts, and **at least one person was killed in Tel Aviv with 94+ wounded**. Israel declared a state of emergency, closed its airspace, called up mass reservists, and moved Prime Minister Netanyahu to a bunker. Thirty-one direct missile strikes were reported across the country.

Iran simultaneously attacked US and allied military installations across the Gulf. Strikes targeted **Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar**, the **US Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain**, **Ali al-Salem Air Base in Kuwait**, and **Al Dhafra Air Base in the UAE**. One civilian was killed in Abu Dhabi from falling missile debris — the first confirmed fatality from an Iranian strike on a Gulf state. Four people died in Suweida, Syria, when an Iranian missile struck a building. Jordan intercepted 49 drones and ballistic missiles. Explosions were reported in Riyadh. Every Gulf state temporarily closed its airspace, and all condemned Iran's retaliatory strikes on their territory. In Iraq, an Israeli missile struck the Kataib Hezbollah headquarters near Baghdad, killing 2 and injuring 8.

## Strait of Hormuz closes as Houthis reactivate Red Sea campaign

The economic consequences may prove as destabilizing as the military action itself. Iran's Revolutionary Guards announced an effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz following the strikes. Ships reported hearing radio broadcasts from the Iranian Navy declaring that "no ship is allowed to pass." UKMTO confirmed receiving multiple vessel reports of the closure but noted these broadcasts "do not constitute a lawful restriction on navigation under international law." Regardless of legality, the practical effect was immediate: **tankers turned around**, shipping paused, and approximately 750 commercial vessels in transit were affected. Greece's shipping ministry advised all Greek-flagged vessels to avoid the entire region. Qatar suspended maritime navigation. Bahrain halted port operations. War risk insurers submitted cancellation notices for Hormuz-area policies.

Oil prices, already elevated at **$72.87/barrel** (Brent) on February 27, face a potential shock when markets reopen Monday. JP Morgan estimates a full Hormuz blockade could push prices to **$120–130/barrel**. Lombard Odier considers a temporary spike to $100+ "plausible." Saudi Arabia and UAE have roughly 2.6 million barrels per day of bypass pipeline capacity, but this covers only a fraction of the 20 million barrels per day that normally transit Hormuz.

Compounding the maritime crisis, **Houthi officials told AP on February 28 that the group will resume missile and drone attacks on Red Sea shipping** in solidarity with Iran. This ends roughly four months of calm since the October 2025 Gaza ceasefire. The announcement effectively kills the shipping industry's tentative return to the Suez Canal route — Maersk had already rerouted vessels back to the Cape of Good Hope on February 27, just weeks after restarting Red Sea transits. Xeneta chief analyst Peter Sand said the developments "shatter hopes of a large-scale return of container shipping to the Red Sea in 2026."

## A coordinated cyberattack plunged Iran into digital darkness

The military strikes were accompanied by what may be the most sophisticated cyber operation in history. NetBlocks confirmed Iranian internet connectivity dropped to **4% of normal traffic levels** — a near-total blackout. Critical infrastructure, official news sites, and IRGC communications systems went offline. State media outlets including IRNA, Tasnim, and ISNA were hacked, with subversive messages displayed against Khamenei on Tasnim's platforms. The Mossad launched a Farsi-language Telegram channel to broadcast news directly to Iranian citizens.

The cyber assault combined electronic warfare disrupting navigation and communications, DDoS attacks, and deep intrusions into energy and aviation data systems. Western intelligence sources indicated the operation specifically targeted IRGC communications infrastructure to prevent coordination of counterattacks and disrupt drone and ballistic missile launches. This built on a campaign that began in January 2026, when government satellite broadcasts were hacked and regime-overthrow messaging was aired to millions of Iranian households. Iran was already operating under degraded internet conditions — authorities had imposed blackouts since January 8 to suppress nationwide economic protests, and even before the strikes, traffic was reduced by 50%.

The cyber dimension extends far beyond this single day. US Cyber Command disclosed on February 4 that it had **digitally disrupted Iranian air defense systems** during the June 2025 Operation Midnight Hammer strikes, preventing surface-to-air missile launches against American aircraft. Israel's National Cyber Directorate reported handling **26,500 cyber incidents in 2025** — a 55% increase — with Israel ranking as the world's #1 target for geopolitically motivated cyberattacks. Iranian cyber operations surged 700% since June 2025, targeting Israeli power grids, hospitals, and civilian applications.

## Proxy forces calculate their next moves

The region's proxy networks are now making critical decisions. **Hezbollah** condemned the strikes and raised its alert level but has stopped short of military action, adopting a "wait and see" posture. The group remains at its weakest point after Israeli attacks decimated its leadership and degraded its arsenal throughout 2024–2025. CFR analysts assess that "Hezbollah has determined it is not in its interest to inject itself into this war" — at least for now. The IDF has deployed mass reservists to the northern border as a deterrent.

**Houthi forces** represent the most likely proxy escalation vector. Beyond announcing resumed Red Sea attacks, they have explicitly framed participation in hostilities as a duty. However, analysts note the Houthis are still recovering from significant US strikes in 2025 and face domestic political constraints — Chatham House observes they "cannot afford to frame a war as one fought simply on behalf of Iran."

The **Gaza ceasefire**, technically in its Phase 2 since January 14, 2026, was already fragile before this escalation. Despite the ceasefire, **618 Palestinians have been killed and 1,640 wounded** since it took effect, with Israel violating it at least 1,620 times according to Gaza authorities. The total confirmed death toll since October 7, 2023 stands at over **72,082**, including 20,179 children. Israeli settlers entered Gaza in late February with a government minister declaring "Gaza will always be ours," while in the West Bank, sweeping annexation measures were approved on February 8. The broader US-Iran conflict now threatens to collapse whatever remains of ceasefire arrangements.

## The international system faces its greatest test in decades

The global response has been swift but divided. France called for an urgent UN Security Council meeting. The UK confirmed British planes are participating in "coordinated regional defensive operations." Russia and China requested an emergency UNSC session, condemning the strikes as an "unprovoked act of armed aggression." Gulf states find themselves in an impossible position — struck by Iranian missiles on their own soil while hosting the US bases that launched the offensive. Oman's foreign minister urged Washington "not to get sucked in further," while Iran's Araghchi signaled willingness to talk "if attacks stop first."

The situation represents a convergence of every major Middle East fault line into a single crisis. A direct US-Iran military confrontation — long considered the region's ultimate escalation scenario — is now reality. The Strait of Hormuz disruption threatens a global energy crisis. Cyber and kinetic warfare are being integrated at unprecedented scale. Over **30,000 US service members** are deployed across the region in the largest force posture since 2003. VP JD Vance insisted there is "no chance" of a years-long war, but the IRGC declared operations "will continue relentlessly."

## Conclusion

February 28, 2026 marks a watershed moment. What distinguishes this crisis from previous Middle East escalations is the **simultaneity of every possible escalation vector activating at once**: direct state-on-state warfare between the US/Israel and Iran, proxy mobilization across Yemen, Iraq, and potentially Lebanon, dual maritime chokepoint disruption at Hormuz and the Red Sea, the most sophisticated combined cyber-kinetic military operation ever conducted, and strikes on sovereign Gulf states that had sought to remain neutral. The coming 48–72 hours will determine whether this becomes a contained military operation or the opening chapter of a wider regional war. With Khamenei's fate unconfirmed, Iran's command structure in question, and global oil markets facing their most severe supply threat since the 1973 embargo, the stakes extend far beyond the Middle East.
